abstract: >-
  <span class="lang-en">Computing how vibrations travel through an architected metamaterial usually means a slow finite-element eigenvalue solve for every candidate design. This work trains a graph neural network to predict that result, the full elastic band structure, directly from the geometry in a single forward pass.<br><br>The unit cell becomes a graph: junctions are nodes, beams are edges, with a few global descriptors of the cell. Node and edge features are lifted to a 768-dimensional space and refined by six edge-aware message-passing layers (GINE), so each junction gradually integrates its six-hop neighbourhood and effectively "sees" the whole cell. Ten learned <em>band queries</em> then pool the nodes by attention, one query per band, into one embedding per band; a residual stack of 1D convolutions along the wavevector axis couples the bands and enforces smooth dispersion. The output is ten bands over 311 k-points along Y-&Gamma;-X-M-&Gamma;, each with its frequency and a three-channel polarisation (one longitudinal, two transverse).<br><br>The key training idea: the simulator labels bands inconsistently across geometries (two near-identical cells can swap "band 7" after an avoided crossing), so a position-by-position loss would only teach the washed-out average. The loss is therefore permutation-invariant, an optimal band-to-band (Hungarian) assignment at every step, penalising the wrong <em>set</em> of curves rather than a relabelling. Shape terms (along-k correlation, slope, amplitude) keep the high bands from flattening, and a second head predicts polarisation on the same assignment.<br><br>Next: a graph VAE for inverse design (band structure &rarr; geometry), and extending the approach to other physical properties.</span><span class="lang-fr">Calculer comment les vibrations se propagent dans un m&eacute;tamat&eacute;riau architectur&eacute; demande d'ordinaire un lent calcul aux valeurs propres par &eacute;l&eacute;ments finis pour chaque design candidat. Dix <em>requ&ecirc;tes de bande</em> apprises agr&egrave;gent alors les n&oelig;uds par attention, une requ&ecirc;te par bande, en un vecteur par bande ; une pile r&eacute;siduelle de convolutions 1D le long de l'axe du vecteur d'onde couple les bandes et impose une dispersion lisse.
